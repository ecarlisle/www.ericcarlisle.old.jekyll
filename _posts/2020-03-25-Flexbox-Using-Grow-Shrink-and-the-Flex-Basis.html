If you've seen one of my FlexBox presentations, you know I'm a huge fan and probably heard the word "awesome" several times in my description. Flexbox is, after all, an extremely powerful, flexible, lightweight display more for creating UI layouts. It can also greatly simplify the creation of responsive design layouts.

One of Flexbox's biggest challenges, however, is that it takes a bit to grok as usage departs from other layout methodologies. Within its functionality is the ability to proportionally shrink and grow child elements as a function of available space. While that explanation is fair, the subtleties of this functionality are often misunderstood. Oh, and they are also awesome!
