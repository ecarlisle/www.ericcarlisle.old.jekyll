The type and number of characters we use for code indention have and probably will always be a source of contention for programmers. Should we use tabs or spaces and if spaces, how many? While some consider it a tired argument, I find it interesting and often comical. Is there a best practice? Does this have more to do with preference and personal efficiency? Is this a Human Factors case as well as a Computer Science one?

This post is not for evangelizing a certain way of indention or for trolling another. Consider it a story of why I chose to indent the way I do. The topic isn't conducive to being a page-turner, so we'll keep it short and use brevity when possible.

Chapter 1. - College : Tabs as a Source of Beer Money

It was the mid-nineties, I was an undergrad, and learned that HTML was lucrative. I learned by studying other people's code and used what I saw - tabs. I wasn't a CompSci major and the only other language I knew was Fortran 77 ("Whaaaaaat?") using spaces. What, there was another way to indent?

Chapter 2. - Post Y2K : I Can Still Use Tabs, Right?

Though the college plan was Sound Recording, I gravitated to a web career. I learned other languages, how to work with data, and things got generally more complicated. I also started seeing more space-indented code. Huh? Why use multiple characters when I could KISS with 1 character? I hadn't yet discovered (or thought to seek out) editors IDEs that auto-indented using spaces, so this was nuts! I resisted and sallied forth with tabs!

Chapter 3. - The Aughts : Kneel Before Zod Space!

Ok, it's sounding like I'm trolling spaces now, but bear with me. As I worked with larger teams, source control, and distributed projects, *poof* spaces seemed to be the thing. At least, that was my perception. I used what my team used and tried to tweak editors to keep things looking readable. That being said, I chose a new preference.

Chapter 4. - Present Day : TabMan becomes TwoSpace!

I currently indent with two-spaces whenever I can. It's a preference because that works better for. My rationale is:

  • I still want to use less characters.
  • Shallow indention is generally more readable for me.1
  • It's easier for me to tell if the number of indents suggests refactoring needs.
  • I can better eyeball where I open and close code. I know editors help with this, but I don't like depending on that.
  • Diffs are easier for me to review in GitHub, BitBucket, Stash.
  • Editors like Sublime Text can make two spaces feel tab-ish.
1. I like bigger indents with Python.

Chapter 5. : Will Chewie Ever Get His Medal?

Do I still want to use a single character to indent? Yes I do, but there's also the need to abide by different project styling requirements. I would love it if:

  1. My editor indented with tabs that were displayed like two-space depth.
  2. I could configure my editor to save files that saved these "short-tabs" as 2-spaces, 4-spaces, or whatever the project required.
  3. I could reopen said code and still use groovy short-tabs.

If someone should speak out in the comments to say "There are 10 editors that do EXACTLY that! What rock have you been living under?", please do so! You'd be my hero!

Bottom line - my choice of indentation is a combination of personal preference, what I feel makes me more productive, and the most manageable fit with existing standards. Do you feel another way? I dig that! Happy coding!
